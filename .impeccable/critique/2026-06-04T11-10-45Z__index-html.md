## Anti-Patterns Verdict

**LLM assessment**: The page no longer looks instantly AI-generated. The biggest previous tells, numbered markers and bulky legal cards on the homepage, are gone. Remaining tell: repeated pastel cards with emoji/icon circles across info tiles and services.

**Deterministic scan**: `detect.mjs --json index.html` returned `[]`. No bundled anti-patterns were detected in the current homepage markup.

**Visual overlays**: No reliable user-visible overlay was injected in this run. Browser evidence was collected through Playwright screenshots and DOM metrics.

## Overall Impression

The site now feels calm, credible, and aligned with the mobility-first positioning. The strongest assets are the real trainer photography, the non-medical copy, and the bilingual structure. The single biggest opportunity is to make consultation booking feel less like an MVP email workaround and more like a confident personal-first process.

## What's Working

1. Real photography creates trust and avoids generic fitness stock energy.
2. The copy is careful: movement freedom, posture, flexibility, control, and routines instead of medical cure promises.
3. Technical hygiene is good: no analytics, no tracking scripts, no visible Calendly copy, no console errors, and no horizontal overflow at 390, 817, or 1440 px.

## Priority Issues

### [P1] Booking still depends on mailto

**Why it matters**: The primary business goal is a free consultation request. If the visitor has no configured email app, the prepared email can fail or feel confusing.

**Fix**: Add either a real booking link when ready or a backend/form service. Until then, add a clearer fallback line near the submit button: "If nothing opens, write directly to ..."

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable harden consultation flow`

### [P2] Card-grid rhythm still dominates the middle

**Why it matters**: The page is calmer than before, but info tiles plus service cards still create a familiar generated-landing rhythm.

**Fix**: Keep pastel cards, but make one middle section photo-led or story-led. The services block could become a compact consultation menu instead of six equal cards.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable layout services section`

### [P2] Mobile form appears late and is long

**Why it matters**: Mobile users must scroll through many sections before reaching the form. The form itself asks for five required inputs.

**Fix**: Add a secondary compact CTA before services or allow the contact section to jump directly to the form. Later, replace email flow with a booking link.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable adapt mobile conversion`

### [P2] Trust gaps remain visible in legal and qualifications

**Why it matters**: "Address to be added," VAT placeholders, and certificate placeholders are acceptable in development but are public-launch blockers.

**Fix**: Collect final address, VAT handling, qualifications, phone/direct contact option if required, and legal review before deployment.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable harden legal trust`

### [P3] Muted text on lavender is slightly low contrast

**Why it matters**: Measured contrast for muted text on lavender is 4.40:1, slightly below the 4.5:1 baseline for normal text.

**Fix**: Darken muted text a little in lavender sections or use the main text color for body copy there.

**Suggested command**: `$impeccable audit contrast`

## Persona Red Flags

**Jordan, first-time visitor**: Jordan understands the offer and sees a calm entry point. Red flag: the exact response time and consultation length are not visible, so the first step still feels slightly undefined.

**Casey, distracted mobile user**: Casey sees strong mobile CTAs, but the actual form is far down the page and requires several fields. High risk: Casey taps CTA, scans, then leaves before completing the email flow.

**Riley, trust-sensitive German visitor**: Riley appreciates Impressum and privacy access. Red flag: placeholders for address, VAT, and qualifications signal that the site is not ready for public launch.
