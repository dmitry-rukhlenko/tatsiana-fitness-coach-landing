# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Adult women and men in Germany who feel stiff or limited in daily movement and want to improve mobility, posture, flexibility, and body control without aggressive fitness pressure.

They may be beginners, returning after a break, sitting a lot during the day, or unsure what kind of training is safe and realistic for their routine. They need a calm first step, clear language, and confidence that the consultation is not a medical promise or a hard fitness challenge.

## Product Purpose

The website is a bilingual DE/EN landing page for Tatsiana Rukhlenko, focused on mobility-first fitness coaching.

Its primary purpose is to build trust and lead visitors to request a free online consultation. Success means a visitor quickly understands who Tatsiana helps, what the consultation covers, why the approach is careful and individual, and how to send a request without confusion.

The site must support the Germany/EU context with clear contact details, Impressum access, privacy information, and no tracking in the MVP.

## Brand Personality

Calm, precise, warm.

The brand should feel personal, attentive, and competent. It should communicate body awareness, freedom of movement, and realistic progress. The voice should avoid hype and should speak plainly about movement, posture, flexibility, everyday routines, and consultation next steps.

## Anti-references

Do not make the site feel like aggressive gym or bodybuilding marketing.

Avoid medical promises, pain-cure language, diagnosis claims, therapy positioning, and any wording that implies guaranteed treatment outcomes.

Avoid cheap template fitness landing pages: generic stock fitness energy, overused transformation tropes, loud before/after pressure, fake urgency, overloaded icons, and generic motivational slogans.

Avoid designs that hide the trainer behind abstract decoration. Real photos of Tatsiana should stay central to trust.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with trust before intensity: the visitor should feel understood before being asked to act.
2. Keep the offer concrete: explain the free consultation, the topics discussed, and the next step without vague fitness promises.
3. Use the trainer's real presence: photography and personal contact should carry credibility more than decorative graphics.
4. Stay careful with health language: frame the service around movement freedom, posture, flexibility, control, and routines, not treatment or cure.
5. Make bilingual navigation frictionless: DE and EN visitors should get the same clear path to consultation and legal information.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA is not a formal requirement for this MVP.

Still preserve practical accessibility basics: readable contrast, visible focus states, keyboard-accessible controls, meaningful image alt text, adequate tap targets on mobile, and reduced-motion handling for users who request it.
