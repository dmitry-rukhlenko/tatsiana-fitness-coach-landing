Priority Issues

P1 Booking still depends on mailto

Why it matters: the primary business goal is a free consultation request. If the visitor has no configured email app, the prepared email can fail or feel confusing.

Fix: add either a real booking link when ready or a backend/form service. Until then, add a clearer fallback line near the submit button: “If nothing opens, write directly to …”.

Suggested command: $impeccable harden consultation flow

P2 Card-grid rhythm still dominates the middle

Why it matters: the page is calmer than before, but info tiles plus service cards still create a familiar generated-landing rhythm.

Fix: keep pastel cards, but make one middle section photo-led or story-led. The services block could become a compact consultation menu instead of six equal cards.

Suggested command: $impeccable layout services section

P2 Mobile form appears late and is long

Why it matters: mobile users must scroll through many sections before reaching the form. The form itself asks for five required inputs.

Fix: add a secondary compact CTA before services or allow the contact section to jump directly to the form. Later, replace email flow with a booking link.

Suggested command: $impeccable adapt mobile conversion

P2 Trust gaps remain visible in legal and qualifications

Why it matters: “Address to be added,” VAT placeholders, and certificate placeholders are acceptable in development but are public-launch blockers.

Fix: collect final address, VAT handling, qualifications, phone/direct contact option if required, and legal review before deployment.

Suggested command: $impeccable harden legal trust

P3 Muted text on lavender is slightly low contrast

Why it matters: measured contrast for muted text on lavender is 4.40:1, slightly below the 4.5:1 baseline for normal text.

Fix: darken muted text a little in lavender sections or use the main text color for body copy there.

Suggested command: $impeccable audit contrast
